Eagles

What sets apart eagles from the other raptor groups is not only their larger size, but also their longer and broader wings. Another characteristic that sets eagles apart from the other raptor groups, is something they don't due while hunting. Most raptors (hawks, falcons, etc...) look behind them, even while attacking. Eagles, for the most part do not. Maybe thats part of the reason eagles are more looked at as more independent than other birds of prey, and why the Bald Eagle became the American symbol.
